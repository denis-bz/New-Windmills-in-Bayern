#### How profitable are windmills in Germany, from EEG tax money ?

"Rated power" (Nennleistung) for a windmill is the *maximum* it can generate,
e.g. 6.2 MW at windspeeds 40 km/h.
Over a year, the electricity actually generated might be 20 % to 40 % of the maximum.
This is called "yield" (Ertrag) or "capacity factor".
It depends, if course, on actual windspeeds at a given site and on turbine type.

In Germany, wind power, solar power etc. are subsidized with around
€20.000.000.000 per year out of the Federal Budget, i.e. tax money; see
[Erneuerbare Energien Gesetz](https://de.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erneuerbare-Energien-Gesetz#EEG-Konto_seit_dem_1._Juli_2022)
. New onshore wind turbine owners get about €70 per MWh from the EEG,
guaranteed for 20 years.
As I understand it, if the market price is 1/4 of that, the taxpayer pays the remaining 3/4.
A sample calculation of profit:
```
  6 MW maximum
* 28 % yield
* €70 EEG price per MWh
* 365 * 24 hours per year
--------
€1.030.176 per windmll per year.
```
But this is just paper. Only windmill owners (and a few politicians and lobbyists)
know the real % yields and € profits from windmills.
